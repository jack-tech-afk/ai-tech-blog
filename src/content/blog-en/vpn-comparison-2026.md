## What Is a VPN and Why Do You Need One?

A VPN creates an **encrypted virtual tunnel** over the internet, making your online activity invisible to third parties.

Here's what a VPN does for you:

- **Encrypts your traffic**: Keeps your data safe even on public Wi-Fi
- **Hides your IP address**: Conceals your location to protect your privacy
- **Bypasses geo-restrictions**: Access content from other countries or regions
- **Prevents ISP tracking**: Stops your internet provider from logging your browsing history

In 2026, with the rise of remote work and increasing cyber threats, **VPN adoption has become essential even for individuals**. With more people using public Wi-Fi than ever, a VPN is no longer "nice to have" — it's a necessity.

## 5 Key Factors for Choosing a VPN

### 1. Connection Speed

VPNs route your traffic through an encrypted tunnel, which inevitably introduces some speed loss. Choosing a VPN that minimizes this impact ensures smooth video streaming and video calls. **A speed reduction of less than 20% is considered excellent.**

### 2. Security & Encryption

Security is the primary purpose of a VPN. Always verify that a service supports **AES-256 encryption** and maintains a **no-logs policy** (meaning they don't store your browsing data). Services audited by third-party firms are even more trustworthy.

### 3. Monthly Pricing

VPN pricing ranges from around $2 to over $10 per month. Most services offer **steep discounts for long-term commitments (2-year plans)**, so if you're serious about using a VPN, going long-term is the way to save.

### 4. Simultaneous Device Connections

If you use a smartphone, PC, and tablet, choose a service that supports **multiple simultaneous connections**. For families, 6 or more simultaneous devices is ideal. Surfshark offers unlimited connections.

### 5. Server Availability in Your Region

If you need to access region-specific content (like Netflix Japan, TVer, or Abema from abroad), check whether the VPN has **servers in the country you need**. More servers generally means faster and more stable connections.
