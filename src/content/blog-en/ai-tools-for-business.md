## How to Get Started: A 3-Step Plan for Beginners

### Step 1: Identify Your Biggest Time Sink

Think about your average workweek. Where do you spend the most time on repetitive tasks? That is where AI will have the biggest impact.

- **Drowning in emails?** → Start with ChatGPT or Copilot
- **Too many meetings?** → Try Otter.ai
- **Struggling with presentations?** → Use Gamma
- **Data analysis headaches?** → Go with Julius AI

### Step 2: Start with One Free Tool

Do not try to adopt five tools at once. Pick the one tool that addresses your biggest pain point and use it for two weeks. Most tools on this list have free tiers, so there is no financial risk.

### Step 3: Measure and Expand

After two weeks, ask yourself: "Am I saving time? Is the output quality good enough?" If yes, consider the paid tier and add a second tool. If not, try a different option from the list.

## Common Mistakes Beginners Make with AI Business Tools

1. **Trying to automate everything at once** — Start small, prove value, then expand
2. **Not reviewing AI output** — AI is a draft generator, not a final-product machine
3. **Using personal plans for business data** — Invest in business tiers for data privacy
4. **Writing vague prompts** — The more specific your instructions, the better the output
5. **Ignoring the free tiers** — Always test before you buy
