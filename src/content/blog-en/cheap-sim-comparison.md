## How to Choose a Budget SIM: 5 Checkpoints

### 1. Monthly Price and Data Allowance

Budget SIM pricing varies significantly based on data allowance. **Check your actual monthly data usage** before selecting a plan.

- **1–3GB** → Home Wi-Fi users who rarely use mobile data
- **5–10GB** → Standard use: social media and web browsing
- **20GB** → Video streaming and tethering users
- **Unlimited** → Those who want to use data worry-free

### 2. Network Speed

Budget SIM speeds vary by network type and time of day. **Speeds tend to drop during lunch (12–1 PM) and commuting hours**, so prioritizing performance during these times is important.

Major carriers' online-only plans (ahamo, LINEMO, povo) use the **carrier's own network infrastructure, resulting in minimal speed degradation** — a major advantage.

### 3. Calling Options

If you make frequent phone calls, compare calling add-ons:

- **5-minute unlimited calling** → For frequent short calls (~¥550–880/month)
- **Fully unlimited calling** → For long conversations (~¥1,100–1,980/month)
- **LINE calls are enough** → No calling add-on needed

Rakuten Mobile offers a unique advantage: **free domestic calls via the Rakuten Link app**.

### 4. Customer Support

Most budget SIMs are primarily online-support. If you need in-store assistance, choose a carrier with **physical retail locations**.

- **Has stores** → Rakuten Mobile, UQ mobile, Y!mobile, mineo
- **Online only** → ahamo, LINEMO, povo, IIJmio

### 5. Network (docomo/au/SoftBank/Rakuten)

Each network has different coverage and quality. **Verify that your area has adequate signal** before committing.

## How to Switch to a Budget SIM: 4 Easy Steps

### Step 1: Get an MNP Transfer Number

Obtain an MNP (Mobile Number Portability) transfer number from your current carrier. Since May 2023, **MNP One-Stop** carriers let you complete the transfer from the new carrier's side — no need to request a number in advance.

**One-Stop compatible carriers:** docomo, au, SoftBank, Rakuten Mobile, ahamo, LINEMO, povo, UQ mobile, Y!mobile, and more.

### Step 2: Apply with the New Carrier

Sign up through the carrier's official website. You'll need:

- Government-issued ID (My Number Card, driver's license, etc.)
- Credit card or bank account information
- Email address

### Step 3: Set Up Your SIM (or Activate eSIM)

For a physical SIM, insert it into your phone after delivery and configure APN settings. **eSIM-compatible phones** can be activated immediately by scanning a QR code after registration.

### Step 4: Verify and Configure

Confirm that calls and data are working correctly, then adjust email and app settings as needed.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Q. Will my connection speed be slower?

Major carriers' online-exclusive plans (ahamo, LINEMO, povo) **run on the carrier's own network, so there's virtually no speed difference**. MVNOs can slow down during lunch and commuting hours, but it's rarely an issue for everyday use.

### Q. Can I keep using my current phone?

In most cases, **you can switch without changing your phone**. However, SIM-locked devices need to be unlocked first (devices purchased after October 2021 are generally SIM-free by default).

### Q. Do I need to transfer my LINE or apps?

If you're only changing SIMs, **your phone's data and apps are unaffected**. No LINE transfer is needed. However, carrier email addresses (@docomo.ne.jp, etc.) require a separate paid portability process.
